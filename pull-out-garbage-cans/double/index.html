How to Choose the Right Double Pull Out

Cabinet width is the first filter. A double bin requires more interior width than a single. The minimum realistic width for a quality double pull out is 15 inches — you won't find a well-built dual-bin system for anything narrower. Measure your interior cabinet width before browsing.

Soft close is worth it. A double pull out is heavier than a single — two bins full of trash can weigh 15–20 lbs. Without soft close, that weight slams the system closed. The Rev-A-Shelf 4WCTM and 53WC both include soft close as standard. For budget picks, consider adding a soft-close damper add-on separately.

Bin size vs. cabinet height. Double pull out systems with two 35qt bins are typically 19–21 inches tall. Make sure your cabinet has enough height from the floor to the shelf above before ordering. If your cabinet is short, look for compact double-bin units with 25qt bins instead.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the minimum cabinet size for a double pull out garbage can?

The minimum realistic interior cabinet width for a quality double pull out is 15 inches. At 12 inches, most double-bin systems are too cramped to function reliably — the bins press against the slides. If you have a 12-inch cabinet, use a single 35qt bin instead and keep a recycling bin elsewhere.

Can I use both bins for trash instead of one for recycling?

Absolutely. Many people use both bins for trash — one for general waste, one for food scraps or compostables. The bins are identical and interchangeable. You can color-code them with different colored bag liners to keep them organized.

Are double pull out systems harder to install than single bins?

Slightly — double systems are heavier and a two-person install is strongly recommended. The process is the same (slide mounting, carriage insertion), but the unit weight makes solo leveling more difficult. Budget 60–90 minutes for a double bin vs. 30–45 minutes for a single.
