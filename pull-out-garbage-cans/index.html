What to Look for in a Pull Out Garbage Can

Cabinet width is the first thing to measure. Most pull out systems are designed for 12-inch, 15-inch, or 18-inch base cabinets — those measurements refer to the interior cabinet opening, not the door width. Measure the interior width at the narrowest point, and subtract about 1 inch for slide clearance.

Capacity matters for household size. A single 35-quart bin works for 1–2 people. For families of 3 or more, look for a double-bin system (2 × 35 qt or larger) so you can separate trash and recycling without opening the cabinet twice.

Mounting type determines how the unit attaches. Bottom-mount systems sit on the cabinet floor on full-extension slides — these are the most common. Top-mount (undermount) systems hang from the shelf above and are ideal when you want to keep the cabinet floor free. Door-mount systems open automatically when you open the cabinet door.

Soft-close slides are worth the upgrade if you have a busy kitchen. The dampened close mechanism prevents slamming and adds a premium feel. Rev-A-Shelf offers soft-close across most of their lineup for around $20–$40 more.

Frequently Asked Questions

What cabinet size do I need for a pull out garbage can?

Most pull out garbage cans require a base cabinet at least 12 inches wide. Standard sizes are 12-inch, 15-inch, and 18-inch cabinets. Always measure your interior cabinet width — subtract about 1 inch from the opening for clearance before choosing a model.

Are pull out garbage cans hard to install?

Most models are DIY-friendly and take 30–60 minutes with basic tools (drill and screwdriver). Top-mount systems attach to the underside of the cabinet shelf; door-mount systems attach directly to the cabinet door. Rev-A-Shelf includes full instructions and hardware.

Do pull out garbage cans work in all cabinets?

Not all cabinets are compatible. You need a base cabinet with the correct interior width and at least 18 inches of depth. IKEA Sektion cabinets require specific adapters — check the Rev-A-Shelf IKEA-compatible line. Face-frame cabinets may need a filler strip for proper fit.

What is the difference between top-mount and bottom-mount pull out garbage cans?

Top-mount systems slide on rails attached to the underside of the shelf above, freeing up cabinet floor space. Bottom-mount systems sit on the cabinet floor on rails and typically hold larger bins. Most kitchen installs use bottom-mount; top-mount is common under sinks with plumbing below.
