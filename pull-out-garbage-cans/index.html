Why Pull Out Trash Cans Are Better Than Traditional Kitchen Bins

Traditional kitchen trash cans have several annoying problems:

  • They take up floor space.
  • They expose odors constantly.
  • They're harder to clean.
  • They make small kitchens feel crowded.

A pull out kitchen trash can solves almost all of these issues. Because the bin stays hidden inside the cabinet, your kitchen instantly looks cleaner and more modern. More importantly, cabinet-mounted garbage systems help contain smells much better than open-standing bins.

One thing I noticed after using pull out systems for years is how much easier maintenance becomes. When food liquids leak at the bottom of the bag — which happens constantly in busy kitchens — you can simply slide the container out and clean everything properly. With regular standalone bins, cleaning becomes messy fast.

Another huge advantage is ergonomics. Soft-close sliding rails make opening and closing the trash compartment smooth and quiet. Many newer systems also include dual compartments for trash and recycling, which helps organize waste without needing multiple visible bins in the kitchen.

For smaller homes or apartments, under cabinet trash cans are especially useful because they maximize usable floor space while keeping the kitchen visually clean.

What to Look for Before Buying a Pull Out Garbage Can

Not all pull out trash systems are built the same. Some are incredibly durable and smooth, while others become frustrating after a few months of daily use. Here's what actually matters before buying one.

Cabinet Size Compatibility

Always measure cabinet width, depth, and height. Many people buy a pull out garbage can without checking dimensions first and realize the sliding system doesn't fit under the sink or conflicts with plumbing. For most kitchens: 12-inch cabinets fit compact single-bin systems, 15-inch cabinets work best for dual bins, and 18-inch or larger cabinets allow heavy-duty systems. If you cook frequently or have a larger family, dual-bin systems are usually worth it. See our guides for 12-inch cabinets, 15-inch cabinets, and 18-inch cabinets for specific recommendations.

Soft-Close Drawer Slides

This feature matters more than you think. Cheap rails feel rough, noisy, and unstable over time. High-quality soft-close slides prevent slamming and make the entire system feel premium. Look for ball-bearing slides, heavy-duty steel rails, and full-extension sliding systems. A smooth sliding mechanism also helps when the trash is heavy. See our dedicated soft-close pull out guide for the best options.

Odor Control Features

Odor control is one of the biggest reasons people switch to pull out trash cans. From experience, smells become unbearable when food liquids leak and stay trapped for too long. In our kitchen, we often had to clean the bin several times a week because leftover liquids created horrible odors fast. The best pull out garbage cans reduce smell using enclosed cabinet storage, tighter lids, removable washable bins, and easy-clean plastic interiors. Some premium systems even include charcoal odor filters.

Single vs Dual Bin Systems

Single-bin systems are ideal for small kitchens, apartments, and minimalist setups. Dual-bin systems work better for recycling separation, families, and heavy cooking households. Personally, dual bins are more practical long term because they help avoid overflowing garbage while keeping recycling organized.

Ease of Cleaning

This is the most overlooked feature. If the plastic interior has awkward corners or the rails are difficult to remove, cleaning becomes annoying very quickly. The best systems have removable bins, washable surfaces, smooth plastic interiors, and accessible rail systems. When dealing with leaking food waste, easy cleaning becomes essential, not optional.

Absolutely. They improve kitchen organization, cleanliness, odor control, and convenience.

What surprised me most after using them for years is how much easier cleaning becomes. Instead of wrestling with a dirty floor-standing trash can, you simply slide the system out, remove the container, clean it thoroughly, and put everything back in place. That alone makes them worth it for many households.

Common Problems With Pull Out Garbage Cans

Weak Sliding Rails

Cheap systems often fail after repeated heavy use. Low-quality rails start wobbling, sticking, or sagging under weight. Always prioritize steel ball-bearing slides. Our under cabinet guide lists the most durable options by cabinet size.

Difficult Cleaning

Some bins look good initially but become frustrating to clean because the container shape traps residue and food particles. Removable bins are essential — don't skip this feature when comparing models.

Bad Odor Buildup

If food liquids leak frequently, odors can build up quickly inside enclosed cabinets. In our kitchen, regular cleaning became necessary at least several times per week because leftover liquids created strong smells fast. Using liners properly and cleaning consistently helps a lot.

Cheap Plastic Bins

Thin plastic cracks over time. Heavy-duty molded containers last significantly longer and are easier to sanitize. This is especially important for double-bin systems that take more daily abuse.

How to Clean a Pull Out Kitchen Garbage Can Properly

To avoid odor buildup:

  1. Remove the trash bag completely.
  2. Pull the container fully out.
  3. Wash the interior using warm water and disinfectant cleaner.
  4. Dry the bin completely before reinstalling.
  5. Clean rails occasionally to remove grease or dust buildup.

One thing I learned quickly is that even premium trash systems smell terrible if moisture stays trapped inside for too long. Cleaning regularly makes a massive difference.

Installation Tips Before Buying

Before ordering:

  • Measure cabinet dimensions carefully.
  • Check for plumbing interference under the sink.
  • Verify slide extension clearance.
  • Decide between side-mount and bottom-mount systems.

If you're not comfortable drilling or aligning rails, professional installation may save time and frustration. For sink-specific installs, see our under-sink pull out guide.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are pull out trash cans easy to install?

Most systems are relatively straightforward if your cabinet dimensions match correctly. Some premium models may require more adjustments. DIY installation typically takes 30–60 minutes with a drill and screwdriver.

Do pull out garbage cans reduce smells?

Yes. Because the trash stays enclosed inside the cabinet, odors spread less compared to open bins. Systems with tighter-fitting lids and removable washable bins perform best for odor containment.

What size cabinet do I need?

Most systems require:

  • 12-inch cabinets for compact single-bin systems
  • 15 inches or more for dual-bin setups
  • 18 inches for large heavy-duty double-bin systems

Always verify measurements before buying — interior width at the narrowest point is what matters.

Are dual-bin systems worth it?

For most households, yes. They simplify recycling separation and help organize waste without needing multiple visible bins in the kitchen. If your cabinet is 15 inches or wider, the upgrade is almost always worth it.

How often should you clean a pull out garbage can?

Ideally 2–3 times per week if you cook frequently or throw away organic food waste regularly. Even premium systems develop odors quickly if moisture stays trapped inside for too long.
