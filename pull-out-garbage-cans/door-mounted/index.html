Best Door Mounted Garbage Can for Kitchen Cabinet (2025)

A door mounted garbage can hangs on the interior face of a cabinet door — no drilling through the cabinet floor, no dedicated pull out hardware, no permanent modification. You mount the bracket on the door itself and the bin rides with the door every time you open it. That makes door mounted garbage cans ideal for renters, under-sink cabinets with plumbing in the way, and any kitchen where there's simply no room for a floor-mount pull out system.

Door Mount vs Pull Out Slide — Which Is Better?

Both systems hide your trash inside a cabinet, but they work very differently. Here's a direct comparison to help you pick the right approach for your kitchen.


Door Mount
  • Installs in minutes — no cabinet floor drilling
  • Renter-friendly (over-door models need zero screws)
  • Works around under-sink plumbing easily
  • Lower capacity — typically 2–6 gallons
  • Extra weight on cabinet door hinges
  • Less stable than a floor-mounted system
Pull Out Slide
  • Higher capacity — 9–13+ gallons standard
  • More stable — mounted to cabinet floor or sides
  • Smoother, more durable long-term
  • Requires drilling into cabinet floor or sides
  • More complex install (30–45 min)
  • May conflict with under-sink plumbing

How to Install a Door Mounted Garbage Can

1

Measure the cabinet door

Check door height, width, and thickness. Most screw-mount bins need a door at least 3/4" thick and tall enough to clear the bin when the door swings open fully. Measure the interior clearance too — the bin must not hit the cabinet shelves or plumbing when the door closes.

2

Choose your mounting method

Over-the-door hooks require zero tools and work on any door edge up to about 3/4" thick. Screw-mount brackets need a drill and 4–6 screws but are far more stable and can support heavier bins. Choose screw-mount for any bin larger than 2 gallons or for heavy-use kitchens.

3

Mount the bracket and attach the bin

For screw-mount: hold the bracket against the inside face of the door, mark the screw holes with a pencil, drill pilot holes to avoid splitting the wood, then drive the screws. Snap or slide the bin into the bracket. For over-the-door: hook the bracket over the door edge and adjust until level.

4

Test the door clearance

Close the cabinet door slowly and check that the bin clears all shelves, pipes, and cabinet walls on all sides. If it catches, adjust the bracket position vertically or switch to a shallower bin. Also check that the door latch still engages properly with the added weight.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will a door mounted garbage can scratch my cabinet door?

Over-the-door hooks can scratch the door edge over time if the rubber pads wear down. Inspect the pads every few months and replace them if they show wear. Screw-mount systems eliminate this problem entirely since the bracket is fixed to the door face and doesn't move against any edge.

How much weight can a door mounted garbage can hold?

Most over-the-door models are rated for 5–10 lbs. Screw-mount systems handle 15–25 lbs depending on the bracket quality and door construction. A standard kitchen trash bag full of food waste weighs 8–12 lbs, so for a primary kitchen bin, use a screw-mount system or empty the bin more frequently if using an over-door hook.

Does a door mounted bin work with soft-close hinges?

It depends on the bin weight. Most soft-close cabinet hinges are rated for doors up to 10–15 lbs. Adding a door-mounted bin increases the load on those hinges. Keep the bin small (under 5 lbs when full) and verify your hinge weight rating. Overloading soft-close hinges will cause them to stop soft-closing and eventually fail.
