How to Install the Rev-A-Shelf 53WC

1

Confirm cabinet dimensions

Interior width must be at least 17 inches (18-inch cabinet). Interior depth must be at least 22 inches. Height from floor to shelf: at least 19.5 inches. Remove any shelf hardware that might interfere with slide clearance.

2

Assemble the bin carriage

The 53WC ships partially assembled. Connect the two bin holders to the main frame using the included bolts. This takes 10–15 minutes and requires a Phillips head screwdriver.

3

Mark and mount the slides

Use the included template to mark slide positions on the cabinet side walls — not the floor. The 53WC mounts to the walls, not the cabinet bottom. Pre-drill with a 3/32" bit and attach slides with included screws. Use a level.

4

Slide in the carriage

With a second person holding the unit level, slide the bin carriage onto the mounted rails. The soft-close mechanism will engage automatically. Push fully in to confirm the damper engages at the back.

5

Drop in the bins and test

Place both bins in their holders. Close and open the unit 10 times to break in the soft-close mechanism. Adjust damper tension using the small dial on each slide if the close is too fast or too slow.

Rev-A-Shelf 53WC — FAQ

Does the 53WC fit a 15-inch cabinet?

No — the 53WC requires at least 18 inches of interior cabinet width. For 15-inch cabinets, look at the Rev-A-Shelf 4WCTM-15BB2, which fits two 35qt bins into a 15-inch opening using a narrower frame design.

Is the soft close adjustable on the 53WC?

Yes. Each slide on the 53WC has a small tension dial that adjusts the damper strength. If the unit closes too aggressively or doesn't fully close, you can tune it with a flathead screwdriver in about 30 seconds.

What trash bags fit the 53WC bins?

Each bin in the 53WC holds 35 quarts — equivalent to an 8-gallon bag. Standard 8-gallon kitchen bags fit well. For extra slack and easier removal, a 10-gallon bag works too. Avoid 13-gallon bags — they'll bunch up inside and smell faster.

Can I use the 53WC for recycling on one side?

Absolutely — this is the primary use case. Many buyers use the left bin for trash and the right bin for recycling. The bins are identical and interchangeable, so you can color-code them with colored bag liners for easy visual sorting.
