1. Hafele Waste Bin Pull Out

$65–$90 12 inch min 2 × 25 qt

The Hafele pull out is the closest budget substitute to the Rev-A-Shelf RV-35. It uses a similar bottom-mount rail system and fits the same 12-inch minimum cabinet. The build quality is lighter — the rail arms are thinner steel — but for a rental property or low-traffic kitchen it holds up fine. Ships from Amazon with standard Prime delivery.

Pros
  • Fits 12-inch cabinets
  • Two bins included
  • Fast delivery
Cons
  • Lighter rails — less durable
  • Smaller 25qt bins
  • No soft close

2. KV 8150 Pull Out Waste Container

$85–$110 15 inch min 2 × 8 gal

KV (Knape & Vogt) is one of the few North American brands that can match Rev-A-Shelf's build quality at a slight discount. The 8150 series uses full-extension slides, a steel frame, and two 8-gallon bins. It's the best alternative if you have a 15-inch cabinet and don't want to pay simplehuman prices.

Pros
  • Solid North American brand
  • Full-extension steel slides
  • Good bin capacity
Cons
  • No soft close option
  • Less widely available
  • Fewer color choices

3. Feoteh Pull Out Trash Can

$45–$70 12 inch min 2 × 10L

The Feoteh is the cheapest viable pull out trash can on Amazon for 12-inch cabinets. The frame is plastic-heavy and the slides are basic, but it works. This is the right buy if you're doing a temporary install, renting, or just want to test whether a pull out system is right for your kitchen before committing to a Rev-A-Shelf.

Pros
  • Lowest price available
  • Easy to install solo
  • Good for trial installs
Cons
  • Plastic frame — not durable
  • Very small 10L bins
  • Slides can stick over time

4. VAUTH-SAGEL Pull Out Bin

$110–$150 18 inch min 2 × 35 qt

VAUTH-SAGEL is a German cabinet hardware brand with a strong European market presence. Their pull out waste systems are a direct alternative to the Rev-A-Shelf 53WC for 18-inch cabinets — they include soft-close slides, two full-size bins, and a steel frame. Slightly cheaper than the 53WC and with a different aesthetic (gray steel rather than white plastic bins).

Pros
  • Soft-close included
  • Steel aesthetic — looks premium
  • Comparable to 53WC specs
Cons
  • Harder to find in the US
  • Fewer spare parts available
  • Less Amazon review history

5. Richelieu Hardware Pull Out Waste

$75–$105 12 inch min 1 × 35 qt

Richelieu is a major North American cabinet hardware distributor with a solid pull out waste line. Their single-bin 12-inch unit is a legitimate alternative to the RV-35 with nearly identical specs. If the Rev-A-Shelf is out of stock or you want a slightly different frame profile, Richelieu is the backup pick.

Pros
  • Fits 12-inch cabinets
  • Solid North American brand
  • Good spare parts availability
Cons
  • Single bin only
  • No soft-close model
  • Smaller selection than Rev-A-Shelf

Bottom Line

If Rev-A-Shelf fits your cabinet, buy Rev-A-Shelf — the brand's quality, availability, and installation support are hard to beat. Use these alternatives when: the RV-35 is out of stock, you have a non-standard cabinet size, or your budget is under $70.

For the absolute lowest price, go Feoteh. For the best budget-to-quality ratio, go KV 8150 or Hafele. For a direct 53WC competitor with soft close, the VAUTH-SAGEL is worth the search.
