Best Garbage Can Cabinet for Kitchen (2025) — Freestanding & Built-In

A garbage can cabinet is one of the smartest upgrades for any kitchen. Whether you choose a freestanding tilt-out cabinet — a standalone furniture piece with a hinged door — or a built-in pull out system mounted inside an existing base cabinet, both solutions hide your trash completely. The right choice depends on your layout and whether you're willing to open an existing cabinet for installation.

Best Freestanding Garbage Can Cabinets

Freestanding tilt-out cabinets require zero drilling and work in any kitchen — including rentals. The door tilts open when pushed, revealing one or two bins inside. Most are built from solid wood, MDF, or moisture-resistant resin. The trade-off: they take up floor space and look best when their finish matches your existing cabinetry.

Best Built-In Pull Out Systems

If you have a spare base cabinet, a built-in pull out system is the cleanest-looking solution — it integrates seamlessly with your existing kitchen, uses your current cabinet door, and typically costs less than a new furniture piece. Rev-A-Shelf is the industry standard, with hardware that fits virtually any base cabinet from 12 to 36 inches wide. What to Look for in a Garbage Can Cabinet

Cabinet width: Measure interior width before buying. Standard base cabinets are 12, 15, 18, or 24 inches wide. Most built-in systems require at least 11 inches of interior clearance.

Bin capacity: A 13-gallon bin is the kitchen standard and fits most 15-inch-wide base cabinets. For a dual trash/recycling setup, you'll need at least an 18-inch cabinet.

Door style — tilt-out vs swing: Tilt-out doors arc toward you in a single motion, common on freestanding units. Swing doors are used on built-in pull out systems, where the cabinet door mounts to the pull out frame. Swing-open is generally smoother and more durable long-term.

Material — wood vs MDF vs resin: Solid wood is premium but heavy. MDF is stable and paintable but can swell near moisture. Resin is waterproof but looks less furniture-grade. For cabinets near the sink, moisture resistance matters.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a garbage can cabinet?

A garbage can cabinet is a dedicated furniture piece or built-in system designed to conceal one or more trash bins. It can be a freestanding tilt-out unit or a pull out system inside an existing base cabinet. Either way, the trash is fully hidden when the door is closed.

Can I convert my existing cabinet into a garbage can cabinet?

Yes — and it's simpler than most people expect. Products like the Rev-A-Shelf RAS-ML-HDSC series mount inside any standard base cabinet in about 30–45 minutes with basic hand tools. The pull out frame attaches to the cabinet sides, and your existing door mounts to the front of the frame, opening automatically when you pull the bin out.

What size garbage can fits in a standard cabinet?

A standard 15-inch base cabinet fits a single 35–50 qt (approximately 9–13 gallon) bin. An 18-inch cabinet can accommodate two 17–21 qt bins side by side for a trash-and-recycling setup. Always measure interior clearance before purchasing a built-in system.
