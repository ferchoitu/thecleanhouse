Why Upgrade

Benefits of Using a Pull Out Recycling Bin

Better Kitchen Organization

A hidden trash cabinet creates a more streamlined kitchen layout. Without freestanding trash cans, counters look cleaner, pathways stay clear, and the overall kitchen feels more integrated and modern. Many homeowners are surprised by how much cleaner everything looks after a single afternoon of installation.

Easier Recycling Habits

Recycling becomes more consistent when a dedicated bin already exists inside the cabinet. Instead of needing separate containers, garage bins, or extra kitchen baskets, everything stays centralized. Having an organized recycling bin built into the cabinet makes it much easier to keep the kitchen clean daily — the habit forms naturally when the system is already in place.

Saves Floor Space

Freestanding trash cans consume valuable room, especially in smaller kitchens. A pull-out system frees up floor space while still providing large-capacity storage. This makes a significant difference in apartments, condos, and any compact kitchen layout.

Keeps Trash Hidden and Reduces Odors

Because the bins stay enclosed inside a cabinet, smells are noticeably less noticeable compared to open trash cans. Some premium systems even include sealed lids, odor-control designs, and antimicrobial bins for extra odor management.

How It Works

How a Rail Pull Out Garbage Can Works

The frame mounts directly inside a lower cabinet — usually under the sink, near the dishwasher, or inside a kitchen island. When you pull the cabinet door, the entire trash system slides outward on rails for easy access. Many newer models include soft-close slides, heavy-duty steel rails, and removable bins, all of which improve durability and daily use.

Buying Guide

How to Choose the Best Pull Out Trash Can With Recycling

Not every system fits every kitchen. Before buying, evaluate cabinet size, bin capacity, slide quality, and installation type.

Cabinet Size Considerations

Always measure cabinet width, depth, and height before purchasing. A small measuring mistake can prevent proper installation entirely.

Cabinet Width Recommended Setup
12 inchesCompact single bin only
13–15 inchesNarrow double-bin (HANKEY style)
15–17 inchesStandard small double bin
18 inchesStandard double bin (Rev-A-Shelf, simplehuman)
21+ inchesLarge-capacity dual system

Soft-Close vs. Standard Slides

Soft-close systems are worth the investment. They operate quieter, move smoother, and show less wear over time. Cheap standard slide systems often become noisy or unstable after extended use — especially in high-traffic kitchens. If you can afford $20–30 more, get the soft-close version.

Best Materials for Durability

The best pull out garbage can with recycling systems use powder-coated steel frames, stainless steel hardware, commercial-grade rails, and removable plastic bins. Avoid low-quality plastic rails. Heavy-duty ball-bearing slides typically last much longer under daily kitchen use.

Single vs. Double Bin

Which to choose?

Single bin: Best for small kitchens, apartments, and low-waste households. Requires less cabinet space (fits 12-inch cabinets).

Double bin: Best for families, recycling separation, and busy kitchens. Eliminates the need for a second visible recycling container. Requires 15–18-inch minimum cabinet width.

Our recommendation: If you have an 18-inch or wider cabinet and want the best long-term result, go with the Rev-A-Shelf RV-18KD. For tighter budgets or narrower cabinets, the VEVOR or HANKEY deliver most of the same benefits at significantly lower cost.
Installation

Best Places to Install a Pull Out Garbage Can

Under the Sink

The most common location because it keeps trash near food prep, dishwashing, and cleanup areas. However, plumbing can reduce available space, so accurate measurements are critical — especially for under-sink pull-outs.

Kitchen Islands

Kitchen islands are excellent for pull-out recycling systems because they centralize waste disposal while cooking. This setup works especially well in open-concept kitchens where traffic flows from multiple directions.

Near Prep Stations

Installing the system close to food prep areas makes cooking cleanup faster and more convenient. Many homeowners don't realize how much time this saves until they start using it daily.

Avoid These

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Choosing the Wrong Cabinet Width

One of the biggest installation mistakes is not measuring correctly. Always account for slide clearance, door hinges, plumbing, and the frame's own dimensions. Even a 1-inch discrepancy can prevent proper installation.

Ignoring Weight Capacity

Double-bin systems can become heavy quickly, especially in larger households. Always check slide weight ratings, rail quality, and frame construction before buying. Most premium systems handle 75–110 lbs; budget options may struggle with fully loaded bins.

Buying Cheap Slide Systems

Low-quality rails tend to wobble, stick, become noisy, and fail prematurely. Investing in heavy-duty soft-close slides usually saves money long term — both in replacement costs and daily frustration.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What size cabinet do I need for a pull out garbage can with recycling?
Most double-bin systems require at least a 15-inch cabinet. 18 inches is the most common and comfortable size, fitting two 35-quart bins. Some compact models like the HANKEY fit cabinets as narrow as 13 inches. Always measure interior width, depth, and height before purchasing.
Are pull out trash cans with recycling worth it?
Yes. They hide both trash and recycling inside a cabinet, eliminate the need for extra freestanding bins, make recycling easier, and improve kitchen organization significantly. Most homeowners notice an immediate, daily difference after installation.
Can I install a pull out trash can under the sink?
Yes, but plumbing can reduce available cabinet space. Always measure accurately and check that the system fits around pipes. Some compact models are specifically designed for under-sink configurations with adjustable frames.
What is the best material for pull out trash systems?
Powder-coated steel frames with heavy-duty ball-bearing slides offer the best durability. Avoid thin plastic rails, which tend to wobble or break after extended use. Soft-close slides are worth the extra cost and typically add just $20–30 to the price.
Do pull out recycling bins reduce odors?
Yes. Since the bins stay enclosed inside the cabinet, odors are noticeably less compared to open trash cans. For best results, look for systems with tight-fitting lids and removable bins you can rinse weekly.
