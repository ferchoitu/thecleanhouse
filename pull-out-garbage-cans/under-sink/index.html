Best Under Sink Pull Out Garbage Can (2025)

The under sink pull out garbage can is the trickiest install in the kitchen — the drain pipe, P-trap, and garbage disposal eat up space that a standard pull out can't work around. These picks are specifically designed or sized to fit next to plumbing, with narrower frames, cutout-friendly bins, and top-mount options that keep the cabinet floor clear.

Why Under Sink Is Different

Under sink cabinets have plumbing. The drain pipe, P-trap, and supply lines take up 8–12 inches of the cabinet interior. A standard bottom-mount pull out will hit the drain assembly. The solutions are: (1) use a top-mount (undermount) system that hangs from the shelf above, keeping the floor clear; (2) use a narrow single bin that fits beside the plumbing; or (3) use a system with a cutout in the bin to slide around the P-trap.

The Rev-A-Shelf RV-35 is the most popular under-sink option because it's top-mount — it installs on the underside of the cabinet shelf above, so the plumbing runs underneath it with no conflict. If you need more capacity, a narrow 12-inch bottom-mount bin placed to one side of the plumbing also works in wider sink bases.
