How to Measure Your Cabinet Before Buying

  1. Measure interior width: Open the cabinet door and measure from the inside of one side wall to the other at the narrowest point. Do not measure the door opening — measure inside the box.
  2. Subtract 1 inch: Most pull out systems need about 1 inch of total clearance. If your interior reads 14.5 inches, you need a 12-inch-rated unit, not a 15-inch.
  3. Measure interior depth: Measure from the back wall to the front face frame or door. You need at least 18 inches of depth for most under cabinet pull out units.
  4. Check interior height: Measure from the cabinet floor to the underside of the shelf above. Most single-bin units need at least 16 inches of clearance; tall 13-gallon units need 18–20 inches.
  5. Note your mount type: Confirm whether you want bottom-mount (slides on cabinet floor) or top-mount (hangs from shelf above). Most kitchens use bottom-mount; under-sink installs often need top-mount due to the plumbing.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the standard size for an under cabinet pull out garbage can?

Most under cabinet pull out garbage cans are designed for 12-inch, 15-inch, or 18-inch base cabinets. These measurements refer to the interior cabinet width — not the door. The most common size sold on Amazon is the 15-inch double-bin unit. If you have a standard 36-inch sink base, you likely have an 18-inch side cabinet available.

How difficult is it to install an under cabinet pull out garbage can?

Installation is DIY-friendly for most homeowners and typically takes 30–60 minutes. You'll need a drill, screwdriver, and a level. The main challenge is ensuring the slides are level — a slight tilt causes the bin to roll open on its own or resist closing. Rev-A-Shelf units include all mounting hardware and a detailed instruction sheet.
